# Sri Lanka Income Tax — Individuals (Year of Assessment 2025/2026)

Income tax in Sri Lanka is administered by the **Inland Revenue Department (IRD)** under the **Inland Revenue Act, No. 24 of 2017** (as amended). Official information and e-filing: **https://www.ird.gov.lk**

## Who must file

- **Resident individuals** with taxable income above the personal relief threshold after allowable deductions.
- **Employees** may have tax deducted via **PAYE**; a separate return may still be required depending on other income.
- **Self-employed persons**, professionals, and business owners must register for a **Tax Identification Number (TIN)** and file returns.
- **Non-resident individuals** earning Sri Lanka–sourced income may have separate obligations.

## Personal relief (2025/2026)

- **Rs. 1,800,000** personal relief for **resident / non-resident citizens** of Sri Lanka (not available for gains from realization of investment assets).
- Additional reliefs may apply (e.g. solar panel relief up to Rs. 600,000 per year under qualifying conditions; rent relief on investment assets — see IRD tax chart).

## Progressive tax rates (resident & non-resident individuals)

| Taxable income slab | Rate |
|---------------------|------|
| First Rs. 1,000,000 | 6% |
| Next Rs. 500,000 | 18% |
| Next Rs. 500,000 | 24% |
| Next Rs. 500,000 | 30% |
| Balance | 36% |

**Other rates (selected):**

- Gains from realization of **investment assets**: **10%**
- Betting/gaming and manufacture or sale of liquor/tobacco: **45%**
- Certain **foreign-currency** service income remitted through a bank: maximum **15%**

## How to file

1. Obtain or verify your **TIN** with IRD.
2. Use **IRD e-Services** (online) for income tax return filing: https://www.ird.gov.lk/en/eservices/
3. Keep supporting documents: employment certificates, bank interest statements, rental income records, business accounts.
4. Pay any **balance tax** due by the statutory due date (check current IRD circular for the year of assessment).

## Common documents

- National Identity Card (NIC) or passport
- TIN certificate / registration proof
- PAYE certificate from employer (if employed)
- Statements for other income (interest, rent, business)
- Prior year assessment / return copy (if applicable)

## Penalties

Failure to file or pay on time may result in penalties and interest under the Inland Revenue Act. Contact IRD or check the website for current penalty rules.

## Where to get help

- **IRD Head Office & regional offices** — see https://www.ird.gov.lk for contact details
- **Taxpayer services** and publications including the annual **Tax Chart**
